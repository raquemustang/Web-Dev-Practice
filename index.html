Flower Symbolism
Gardenias symbolize purity, innocence, and love, with a strong, deep-rooted association with secret admiration and refinement. They represent joy, trust, and clarity.
Tulips broadly symbolize perfect, deep love, rebirth, and charity, often representing the arrival of spring and new beginnings. As one of the first flowers to bloom in spring, they also represent rebirth, new beginnings, and charity.
The meaning of Hydrangeas varies culturally. In Japan, they signify heartfelt emotion and apology, while in Europe, they symbolise arrogance and vanity, with a historical belief linking them to singlehood.
Sunflowers primarily symbolize unwavering faith, adoration, loyalty, and longevity due to their heliotropism and sturdy growth; often used to convey positivity and friendship.
Peonies are rich, versatile symbols representing prosperity, good fortune, honor, and romance. Known as the "King of Flowers" in China, they signify wealth and high status. In Western cultures, they often symbolize a happy marriage, bashfulness, and deep, passionate love.
